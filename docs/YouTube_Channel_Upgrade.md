## 6. Banner Concept

**Theme:** Dark terminal / globe-intel aesthetic — matches ContentForge and Panopticon Earth.

**Dimensions:** 2560 × 1440px (YouTube safe zone for all devices: center 1546 × 423px)

**Design direction:**

```
Background: #0f0f11 (near-black)

Left side (desktop visible):
  - Faint green terminal grid lines or a subtle wireframe globe
  - A small "< >" or "{ }" bracket motif in teal (#5ec8a0)

Center safe zone (all devices see this):
  - "Dan DeBugger" in bold, slightly condensed sans-serif
    Color: white (#ffffff) with purple glow (#7c6af7)
  - Tagline below in smaller text, color #5ec8a0:
    "builds things • ships in public • sometimes extra fries"

Right side (desktop visible):
  - Faint ContentForge logo or a terminal cursor blinking motif
  - Subtle code lines (opacity ~15%) in purple
```

**Tools to make it:**
- Canva (free, has YouTube banner template at 2560×1440)
- Figma (free, more control)
- Both have dark themes — start from a blank dark canvas, not their default templates

**Colors to use:**
- Background: `#0f0f11`
- Accent purple: `#7c6af7`
- Accent teal: `#5ec8a0`
- Text: `#ffffff`

## 9. What to Do With the 4 Old Crossout Videos

Three options:

| Option | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Unlist them** | Best default — they still exist, links still work, but they don't appear on your channel page |
| **Delete them** | Clean slate, but you lose the watch history and any backlinks |
| **Keep public** | Only if you're going for "authentic origin story" angle |

**Recommendation:** Unlist all 4. They're gaming clips from 4 years ago on a channel
you're rebranding as a dev/coding channel. They create confusion, not nostalgia.

To unlist: YouTube Studio → Content → click each video → Details → Visibility → Unlisted → Save

## 10. First Video to Post (Suggestion)
